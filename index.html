The World is Listening.

In the fast-paced, always-on world of the 21st century media, healthy global reputations require more than influence; they need control.

We created Tricuro to help our clients — national governments, corporations, NGOs and public bodies — to take control of their image and reputation in the global marketplace.

We do this by uniquely combining public diplomacy, public affairs, lobbying, public relations, and social media in a fully-integrated approach that delivers significant results for our international clients.

Our Services

Public Diplomacy

Helping Governments communicate complex and diverse stories to global audiences, advance national interests and overcome reputational challenges among the international community.

Public Affairs

Working with clients to influence government decision-makers and advisers — at the local, state, and federal level — in order to achieve clear public policy objectives.

Public Relations

Creating integrated strategies — incorporating a bespoke mix of message development, media relations, government relations, and event management — to help clients reach their target audiences and achieve their commercial objectives.

Social Media

Using a combination of search engine optimization, social media platforms, and digital content (text, video, and photographs) to encourage positive conversations with target communities and customers.

Global Perspectives on Local, Regional and Global Challenges.

What others say about you in any one part of the world is heard and acted upon everywhere.

Based in Washington DC, Tricuro provides a truly global perspective on reputational challenges. We specialize in helping our clients influence the Internet search space, but we also recognize the continuing importance of maintaining close personal relationships with the traditional international press and broadcasting media; with political decision-makers; and with so-called grassroots activists – wherever in the world they are located.

Tricuro’s international team of strategic communications advisers – drawn from the worlds of business, technology, politics, and the media – has experience of working in a wide range of nations, cultures, languages and internal political situations that makes us uniquely suited to a broad spectrum of reputational challenges.

Digital Control.

Tricuro is a consultancy born to the digital world: we are native to the challenges, strategies and techniques of the online world.

We provide some of the most sophisticated online reputation management strategies available on the market today. Our digital approach aims for the complete control of how the Internet views our clients. Targeting results on Google and other search engines, we aim for complete control of all user editable platforms, giving our clients maximum peace of mind when it comes to their online reputation.

Tricuro's highly experienced team of professionals has tackled the world's biggest Internet search problems - from national identity, to political situations, to high profile and extremely high volume commercial search streams.

Our Work.

Tricuro’s clients include global brands and governments from across North America, Europe, Asia, Africa, and the Middle East. Here are some examples of our recent work:

  • Designing and implementing a discrete but highly effective media relations, congressional, grassroots and public affairs campaign for a global commodities trading company following its purchase and renovation of a major port facility in the USA with significant local and environmental sensitivities.
  • Devising a US market entry communications strategy for a major European aviation brand seeking to win contracts in North America.
  • Conducting a multifaceted public outreach campaign to promote a Middle East country using online reputation management, media outreach and a comprehensive messaging platform, telling that country’s story in a new and engaging way to audiences in the Middle East, Europe and the United States.
  • Delivering a successful media training and media relations program on behalf of a federal government in advance of the G20 and B20 summits held in Los Cabos, Mexico in June 2012, securing significant broadcast and print interviews for their spokesmen in global media outlets.
  • Working with a national government of Asia to communicate the country’s post-conflict humanitarian efforts to the US media and policy community.
  • Helping an international NGO to build a larger and more diversified client base using corporate communications, media relations, events and online reputation management.
  • Providing a satellite telecommunications company with political and media intelligence during a potentially damaging public debate in the US media about a significant business deal.
  • Helping an Embassy to enhance and exploit its relations with Congress, the media, and grassroots supporters in preparation for a series of significant meetings at the United Nations General Assembly.

Tricuro: Our Team

David Sowells

Principal

David is an expert in international strategic communications. He has advised numerous governments, corporations and non-profits including HSBC on political engagement, Trafigura on grassroots and media relations, and the International Executive Service Corps on social media and corporate relations.

David has held a series of senior positions in international communications agencies including the Bell Pottinger Group and Fleishman Hillard. He also served as Public Affairs Director of CNN International and AOL Time Warner Europe, and was Vice President of the UK Satellite and Cable Broadcasters Group. He started his career as an economic adviser in the House of Commons.

Having completed his studies at the London School of Economics and Harvard Business School, David has run successful businesses in Washington DC, London and Brussels.

Chuck Conconi

Chairman, Advisory Board

Journalist and author, Chuck Conconi has more than 40 years experience in television, radio and print journalism.

A veteran journalist, Chuck was editor-at-large of The Washingtonian, and previously an editor of the “Style” section and author of the daily “Personalities” (now “Reliable Source”) column at The Washington Post. He has worked extensively on radio and television on such networks as FOX, NBC and CNN International.

Chuck has written two books (The Energy Balloon and The Washington Sting); was a Press Secretary in the US Senate; and has served as a visiting lecturer at the University of Maryland, Georgetown University, American University, George Washington University and the University of Miami in Florida. He currently serves as Chairman of Qorvis.

Charles N. Andreae, III

Government Relations

Chip Andreae is a strategic business consultant with expertise in political affairs and political risk management. Chip has had extensive exposure to the Washington establishment, with particular focus on foreign policy. He worked for 15 years on Capitol Hill, including 4 years with the Senate Select Committee on Intelligence and 5 years as Chief of Staff for Senator Richard Lugar. As Chief of Staff, Chip led numerous Senate staff delegations to the Near East, Latin America and Africa. Working with the International Republican Institute, Chip helped train newly created political parties for two years in Bulgaria, two years in Romania, and two years in South Africa. He also has served on over a dozen election observer missions around the world on behalf of the U.S. Government.

Previous roles include working for two years as Vice President and Partner of the RCF Group, a Washington-based consulting firm, and a Visiting Lecturer at the Edmund A. Walsh School of Foreign Service at Georgetown University.

Chip holds an M.A. in Government from Georgetown University and a B.A. in Political Science and History from Denison University.

Joey Nichols

Public Affairs, Events, Fundraising

Joey has 20 years experience of public affairs, fund-raising, and event management, giving her a deep understanding of how the worlds of government, politics, non-profit and public opinion intersect.

Luke Chauveau

Strategic Communications, Media Relations

Luke is a specialist in strategic, media and government communications. Based in London, and a Partner of Bell Pottinger Geo-Political, Luke’s clients have included Presidents, Prime Ministers, Ambassadors and governments across the Middle East and Africa, including President Yar’adua of Nigeria, President Banda of Zambia, and the governments of the UAE and Gabon.

Sandra Charles

Government Relations and Foreign Policy

Sandra Charles advises foreign governments on their relations with the US Government. She is also a consultant to US and foreign companies on international marketing opportunities.

Ms. Charles served the US Government for many years as a member of the National Security Council (NSC) staff of the White House and in the Office of the Secretary of Defense, International Security Affairs (OASD ISA). 

Her many career highlights include advising Presidents Reagan and Bush and their senior staff on politico-military issues and programs in North Africa, the Middle East, Southwest and South Asia, including Operation Desert Storm. She was also a key player in forging the Reagan Administration's policy toward the Iran-Iraq War, including the U.S. military operations to protect Kuwaiti shipping in the Gulf in 1987-1988.

In addition to receiving 15 outstanding or superior performance awards, Ms. Charles was presented with the Secretary of Defense Medal of Meritorious Civilian Service in 1988 for her service in the Defense Department. Awarded an OSD scholastic fellowship, she  earned her Masters Degree in International Public Policy at John Hopkins University's School of Advanced International Studies. In conjunction with her work, she has traveled extensively throughout the Middle East, South Asia and East Africa.

Ms. Charles currently serves as Senior Advisor to Dentons and is a member of its Global Business Intelligence Unit.

Tricuro: Advisory Board

Bill McSweeny

Advisory Board

Bill McSweeny has had a long and distinguished career, culminating in his appointment as President of Occidental International Corporation, which he helped grow into a major force in its industry.

Bill started his career as an infantryman in the Korean War, becoming an award-winning war correspondent in Vietnam and Israel, and later joined the staff of President Lyndon Johnson, winning two special awards for service.

Four Presidents have appointed Bill to various posts, including UNESCO, Special Envoy to the USSR, Special Ambassador to Bolivia, Special Ambassador to Oman, President's Representative to Korea for the 50th anniversary ceremonies, Co-Chair of the NATO 50th Anniversary at the White House and a trustee of the Kennedy Center.

Long active in promoting human rights, Bill led the successful negotiations to gain release of "Refuseniks" in the Soviet Union and was much involved in medical rescue efforts at Chernobyl and during the Armenian earthquake.

Among numerous achievements, Bill created Washington Episcopal School along with his wife Dorothy, is a trustee of the American Air Museum in England, and was Chair of Ford's Theater. He has written three books, served on a number of arts boards, and was recently awarded the title "Washingtonian of the Year."

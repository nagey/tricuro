Global Perspectives on Local, Regional and Global Challenges.

What others say about you in any one part of the world is heard and acted upon everywhere.

Based in Washington DC, Tricuro provides a truly global perspective on reputational challenges. We specialize in helping our clients influence the Internet search space, but we also recognize the continuing importance of maintaining close personal relationships with the traditional international press and broadcasting media; with political decision-makers; and with so-called grassroots activists – wherever in the world they are located.

Tricuro’s international team of strategic communications advisers – drawn from the worlds of business, technology, politics, and the media – has experience of working in a wide range of nations, cultures, languages and internal political situations that makes us uniquely suited to a broad spectrum of reputational challenges.

Our Work

Tricuro’s clients include global brands and governments from across North America, Europe, Asia, Africa, and the Middle East. Here are some examples of our recent work:

  • Designing and implementing a discrete but highly effective media relations, congressional, grassroots and public affairs campaign for a global commodities trading company following its purchase and renovation of a major port facility in the USA with significant local and environmental sensitivities.
  • Devising a US market entry communications strategy for a major European aviation brand seeking to win contracts in North America.
  • Conducting a multifaceted public outreach campaign to promote a Middle East country using online reputation management, media outreach and a comprehensive messaging platform, telling that country’s story in a new and engaging way to audiences in the Middle East, Europe and the United States.
  • Delivering a successful media training and media relations program on behalf of a federal government in advance of the G20 and B20 summits held in Los Cabos, Mexico in June 2012, securing significant broadcast and print interviews for their spokesmen in global media outlets.
  • Working with a national government of Asia to communicate the country’s post-conflict humanitarian efforts to the US media and policy community.
  • Helping an international NGO to build a larger and more diversified client base using corporate communications, media relations, events and online reputation management.
  • Providing a satellite telecommunications company with political and media intelligence during a potentially damaging public debate in the US media about a significant business deal.
  • Helping an Embassy to enhance and exploit its relations with Congress, the media, and grassroots supporters in preparation for a series of significant meetings at the United Nations General Assembly.

David Sowells

Founding Partner

David is an expert in international strategic communications. He has advised numerous governments, corporations and non-profits including HSBC on political engagement, Trafigura on grassroots and media relations, and the International Executive Service Corps on social media and corporate relations.

David has held a series of senior positions in international communications agencies including the Bell Pottinger Group and Fleishman Hillard. He also served as Public Affairs Director of CNN International and AOL Time Warner Europe, and was Vice President of the UK Satellite and Cable Broadcasters Group. He started his career as an economic adviser in the House of Commons.

Having completed his studies at the London School of Economics and Harvard Business School, David has run successful businesses in Washington DC, London and Brussels.

Chuck Conconi

Chairman, Advisory Board

Pulitzer-nominated journalist and author, Chuck Conconi has more than 40 years experience in television, radio and print journalism.

A veteran journalist, Chuck was editor-at-large of The Washingtonian and an editor of the “Style” section and later wrote the daily “Personalities” (now “Reliable Source”) column at The Washington Post. He has worked extensively on radio and television on such networks as FOX, NBC and CNN International.

Chuck has written two books (The Energy Balloon and The Washington Sting); was a Press Secretary in the US Senate; and has served as a visiting lecturer at the University of Maryland, Georgetown University, American University, George Washington University and the University of Miami in Florida. He currently serves as Chairman of Qorvis.
